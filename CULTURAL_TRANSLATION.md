# Cultural Translation Rules For Dayou

## Core Framing

- Translate Chinese metaphysics as a reflection tool, not as absolute prophecy.
- Lead with a modern life tension: uncertainty, relationships, timing, burnout, ambition, boundaries.
- Keep the tone grounded, elegant, and slightly mystical without sounding cultish or fatalistic.
- Make the content usable in under 60 seconds.

## Audience Assumptions

- The viewer is curious about Eastern wisdom but does not know technical Chinese philosophy vocabulary.
- The viewer cares more about clarity and emotional resonance than textual fidelity.
- The viewer may be skeptical of anything that sounds like fortune-telling.

## Translation Rules

- Explain terms before naming them. Example: say "a pattern of change" before saying "hexagram."
- Prefer plain English over transliteration unless the Chinese term itself is the hook.
- Translate for emotional meaning first, then add the classical phrase as a layer of authority.
- Do not over-explain cosmology, dynasties, or line-by-line commentary in short-form scripts.

## What To Avoid

- Do not promise outcomes, lucky days, soulmate certainty, or guaranteed manifestations.
- Do not use fear-based wording like "bad omen," "curse," or "disaster energy."
- Do not present classical texts as rigid rules people must obey.
- Do not sound like a textbook, a temple sermon, or a generic wellness influencer.

## What To Emphasize

- Patterns instead of predictions
- Self-awareness instead of superstition
- Timing instead of urgency
- Inner posture instead of external control
- Choice, responsibility, and reflection

## Script Conversion Rules

- `HOOK`: one sharp insight, contradiction, or emotional tension
- `BODY`: three short beats max; each beat should connect the source text to a modern situation
- `CTA`: invite reflection, comment, or follow; never hard-sell
- `CAPTION`: concise summary, one classical line if useful, then hashtags

## Platform Rules

- TikTok and Instagram Reels should feel conversational, not academic.
- One script should focus on one insight only.
- Assume subtitles will be on-screen, so the spoken copy should be clean and rhythmic.
- If a line sounds beautiful but unclear in English, simplify it.
